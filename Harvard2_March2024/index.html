Simulation-based inference: theory


Generating simulations is sampling from the joint distribution, i.e., ${D} = \{x_i, \theta_i\} \sim p(x, \theta)$

Q. How to use this for inference?
Density estimation problem!

Learn the likelihood distribution.
Model the likelihood with a parametric distribution $q$$_\phi$, e.g., normalizing flow

Fit $\phi$ to match the likelihood $q_{\phi}\rightarrow p(x|\theta)$ $$ \phi^* = \max_{\phi} \sum_i \log q_{\phi}(x_i | \theta_i) \quad \quad \{x_i, \theta_i\} \sim p(x, \theta)$$
Or
Learn the posterior $\,q_{\phi}\rightarrow p(\theta|x) :$ $$ \phi^* = \max_{\phi} \sum_i \log q_{\phi}(\theta_i | x_i) \quad \quad \{x_i, \theta_i\} \sim p(x, \theta)$$

Score matching for Variational Inference

Approximate the posterior $ p(\theta | x) \approx q_{\phi} (\theta) $ by minimizing a dissimilarity metric $d(q;p)$. $$\nabla_{\theta} \log q_{\phi^*}(\theta) = \nabla_{\theta} \log p(\theta, x) \quad \forall \theta \in \Theta$$
Exact score matching
At iteration $t$, sample $\theta_t$, and update $\phi$:
$$\quad \phi_{t+1} = \textrm{min KL}(q_{\phi_t} || q_{\phi_{t+1}})$$ such that $\quad \nabla_\theta \log q_\phi(\theta_t) = \nabla_\theta \log p(\theta_t, x)$.

Closed form updates for $q_\phi = \mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma)$
$\rightarrow$ No hyper-parameters to tune.
Batch and Match
For a batch of $B$ samples: \begin{align} \mathcal{L(q)}: \frac{1}{B}\sum_i ||\nabla_\theta \log q_\phi(\theta_i) - & \nabla_\theta \log p(\theta_i, x) ||^2_{\textrm{Cov}(q)}\\ &+ \frac{2}{\lambda_t}\textrm{KL}(q_{\phi_t} || q_{\phi}) \end{align} Closed form updates for $q_\phi = \mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma)$
Exponential convergence for Gaussian target as $\textrm{limit}\, B\rightarrow \infty$

Reconstructing the Universe with Bayesian inference

High dimensional Bayesian inference

Hamiltonian Monte Carlo (HMC)

Goal: Generate samples from the posterior $p(\theta|x)$.

Construct a Markov chain by simulating Hamiltonian dynamics.
$\theta$: positions; $\,$$u$: auxiliary momentum

Transition kernel: $(\theta, u)\rightarrow(\theta', u')$
$ \dot{\theta} \propto u$
$ \dot{u} \propto -\nabla_{\theta}\log p(x, \theta)$

ODEs evolved using symplectic integrators like leapfrog.
Metropolis-Hastings criterion for accepting proposals $\alpha = 1 \land \frac{p(\theta'|x)}{p(\theta|x)}$

https://github.com/chi-feng/mcmc-demo
Variational inference (VI)

Goal: Approximate the posterior with a parametric distribution $$ p(\theta | x) \approx q_{\phi} (\theta) $$
E.g. $q_\phi = \mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma)$, then $\phi = (\mu, \Sigma)$

Fit parameters $\phi$ to minimize a dissimilarity metric $d(q;p)$
E.g., reverse Kullback-Leibler divergence $$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q||p) = \mathbb{E}_{q}(\log q(\theta) - \log p(\theta|x)) $$

Delayed rejection Hamiltonian Monte Carlo (DRHMC)


When faced with a rejection, make another proposal with a different transition kernel.

Hamiltonian-Monte Carlo:
$F_1:$ Hamiltonian dynamics by leapfrog integration with step-size $\epsilon$ for $L$ steps
Accepts proposals with probability: $ \alpha_1(x\rightarrow y) = \textrm{min}\Big(1, \frac{p(y)}{p(x)}\Big)$
If reject, fall back to $x$
Acceptance probability of DRHMC proposals: $ \alpha_2(x\rightarrow y) = \textrm{min}\Big(1, \frac{p(y)}{p(x)}$ $\frac{1 - \alpha_1(y)}{1 - \alpha_1(x)}$$\Big)$

Score matching for Variational Inference


Approximate the posterior $ p(\theta | x) \approx q_{\phi} (\theta) $ by minimizing a dissimilarity metric $d(q;p)$.


Minimizing $D_{KL}(q||p)$ reverse KL divergence
$\rightarrow$ need samples from $q_\phi$, which is updating.

Minimizing $D_{KL}(p||q)$ forward KL divergence
$\rightarrow$ requires samples from the distribution.
｝
Stochastic gradient descent
Difficult opimization exercise


A different approach: score matching
$$\nabla_{\theta} \log q_{\phi^*}(\theta) = \nabla_{\theta} \log p(\theta, x) \quad \forall \theta \in \Theta$$ if and only if, $\, q_{\phi^*}(\theta) = p(\theta | x) $

  • No constraint on samples being drawn from $p$ or $q$.
  • Matching vectors instead of scalars
    $\rightarrow$ size of vectors $\propto$ dimensionality.

Gaussian Score matching for Variational Inference (GSM)


Algorithm
At iteration $t$,
  1. Sample $\theta_t$
  2. Update $\phi$:
    $$\quad \phi_{t+1} = \textrm{min KL}(q_{\phi_t} || q_{\phi_{t+1}})$$ such that $\quad \nabla_\theta \log q_\phi(\theta_t) = \nabla_\theta \log p(\theta_t, x)$.


For Gaussian distribution $q_\phi = \mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma)$, closed form updates
$$\mu_{t+1} = \mu_t + A_t\big(\nabla_\theta \log p(\theta_t, x) - \nabla_\theta \log q_\phi(\theta_t)\big) $$ $$\Sigma_{t+1} = \Sigma_t + (\mu_t - \theta_t) (\mu_t - \theta_t)^{T} - (\mu_{t+1} - \theta_{t}) (\mu_{t+1} - \theta_{t})^{T}$$

Unlike gradient descent optimization:
  • No hyperparameter to tune, e.g., learning rate
  • Non-local updates, no Taylor approximation.

Batch and Match (BaM)



Match score only at one point
$\rightarrow$ marginal gains from large batch size
$\rightarrow$ parallelization on GPUs



Gaussian variational family $\, q=\mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma)$:

  • No Taylor approximation.
  • closed form updates for $\mu, \Sigma$ to achieve global minimum.
  • Provable exponential convergence for Gaussian target as $\textrm{limit}\, B\rightarrow \infty$



BaM objective:

For a batch of $B$ samples: $$ \mathcal{L(q)}: \frac{1}{B}\sum_i ||\nabla_\theta \log q_\phi(\theta_i) - \nabla_\theta \log p(\theta_i, x) ||^2_{\textrm{Cov}(q)} + \frac{2}{\lambda_t}\textrm{KL}(q_{\phi_t} || q_{\phi})$$
